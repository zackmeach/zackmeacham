# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary reader is a hiring decision-maker evaluating Zack Meacham for a software role: an engineering manager, a technical recruiter, a founder, or a senior engineer on an interview loop. They arrive skeptical and time-poor, often from a LinkedIn or GitHub link, frequently on a phone between other tabs. They are scanning for signal in under a minute: Is this person senior enough? Can they actually ship? Is the AI fluency real or buzzword decoration? Do the claims hold up?

A secondary reader is a peer or collaborator (open-source, civic-tech, a potential co-founder) checking whether Zack is the real thing before reaching out.

The job to be done: let a busy evaluator reach a confident "yes, worth a conversation" fast, and give them defensible evidence they can repeat to a hiring committee.

## Product Purpose

A working portfolio and digital resume for a mid-career software/systems engineer. It exists to convert a cold visitor into an interview or a reply. Success is the visitor leaving able to say three concrete things about Zack without re-reading: the breadth (systems + applied AI + product), the depth (mission-critical ownership under real constraints), and the proof (shipped work they could click into).

It must read as the portfolio of a **mature, tasteful engineer**: confident without hype, precise without coldness, evidence over assertion. The site itself is a work sample. Sloppy craft here contradicts the claim of engineering judgment.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **precise, grounded, quietly confident.**

Voice: calm and declarative. States scope and ownership plainly, then stops. Claims less than it could and lets specifics carry the weight ("built from zero to production-facing daily use" beats "passionate about quality"). Technical without jargon-for-its-own-sake. Never breathless about AI; treats frontier tooling as a practiced instrument, not a personality.

The visual identity is an **engineering-drawing / drafting** metaphor: registration marks at the page corners, a title-block footer (DRAWN BY / SHEET / REV), faint coordinate gutter ticks, dimension callouts. This is personal to a systems engineer and is the site's distinguishing POV. It is the through-line that earns the right to use an editorial-typographic shape without reading as a template.

## Anti-references

- **"AI made this" portfolios.** Generic SaaS-cream landing pages, gradient-text headlines, hero-metric templates, identical icon-heading-text card grids, glassmorphism. If a visitor's first thought is "which template is this," it has failed.
- **The undifferentiated editorial-typographic template.** Display-serif headline + uppercase mono labels + ruled separators is a saturated 2026 lane. This site sits adjacent to it on purpose, but the drafting metaphor must be visibly load-bearing, not decoration, or it collapses into that template.
- **The "modernization engineer" narrow brand.** Zack is not a modernization specialist. Top-level identity copy must lead with breadth (systems + applied AI + product across several very different high-stakes projects). The word "modernization" is accurate inside the Boeing drilldown only; it must never become a top-level drumbeat or a recurring headline word.
- **Hype and buzzword inflation.** No "passionate," "ninja," "rockstar," "synergy," "leverage cutting-edge." No claiming depth that the evidence does not back. Overstatement reads as junior.
- **Em dashes.** Never. Use commas, colons, semicolons, periods, or parentheses.

## Design Principles

1. **Claim less, demonstrate more.** Every assertion should be backed by a specific, checkable artifact (a repo, a role, a shipped outcome). Prefer concrete nouns and past-tense verbs of ownership over adjectives of self-description.
2. **Breadth is the headline; depth is the drilldown.** The top level sells range across systems, applied AI, and product. Individual case studies go deep on one thing. Never let one domain word dominate the identity layer.
3. **Public-safe by default.** Professional work is written to show scope, ownership, and technical depth without exposing sensitive program detail. Internal Boeing codenames (the flagship suite, the instrumentation overhaul, the calibration suite) are described functionally, never named. Specific station counts and team sizes stay vague. A rough dollar figure may appear only if fully untethered from any traceable program context.
4. **The craft is the argument.** The site is itself a work sample for an engineer who claims judgment and taste. Visual precision, copy discipline, accessibility, and clean responsive behavior are not polish; they are the evidence.
5. **Scannable for the skeptic.** A time-poor evaluator must extract breadth + depth + proof in under a minute of scanning, on a phone, without reading full paragraphs. Hierarchy and entry points serve that scan.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body and label text must meet 4.5:1 contrast against the warm paper background (verify the graphite and ink-soft tokens specifically). All interactive elements need visible keyboard focus indicators and a logical tab order. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for every entrance animation and the dimension-callout draw. Maintain a correct single-h1-per-page heading hierarchy. The print/PDF path (resume) must remain high-contrast black-on-white. No information conveyed by color alone.
